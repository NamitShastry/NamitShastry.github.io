Indian National Trust for Art and Cultural Heritage (INTACH)

In Grade 10, I earned national recognition as the recipient of the Best Prize Entry in a nationwide competition organized by the Indian National Trust for Art and Cultural Heritage (INTACH). My work was subsequently featured in the Young INTACH Newsletter (Vol. 20 No. 4, October–December 2023), a publication circulated among students, educators, and heritage advocates across India.

Responding to the United Nations' designation of 2023 as the International Year of Millets, I developed a research-driven submission examining how traditional millet cultivation can address some of the most pressing challenges facing modern societies, including food security, nutritional deficiencies, climate resilience, and sustainable agricultural development. By connecting cultural heritage with contemporary policy and sustainability concerns, I sought to demonstrate how indigenous knowledge systems can contribute to solutions for global challenges.

Being awarded the highest distinction among participants from across the country at the age of fifteen was a meaningful affirmation of my ability to conduct independent research, synthesize interdisciplinary ideas, and communicate complex issues to a broader audience. More importantly, the experience strengthened my conviction that thoughtful analysis and effective communication can play a meaningful role in advancing solutions to challenges of national and global significance.
